## THE CORE TENSION

Immense depth rendered with radical restraint. Not minimalist — dense but quiet. These are very different things. The iceberg model: 90% below the waterline.

The brand personality: understated, curious, intentional, precise, passionate in specific moments. Patient, solitary, then explosive when it matters.

---

## THE FOUR PILLARS (how they govern design decisions)

### California — the atmosphere
Governs: light, space, how things feel perceptually. Fog spectrum, spatial openness, luminous neutrals. The page should feel like it has air and natural light. California is why the brand isn't heavy or somber — everything is aired out.

### Sundarbans — the material
Governs: substance, weight, symbolic vocabulary. The tiger icon, the threshold principle (land meets water), the mangrove ecosystem. When content appears, it should feel substantial and physically placed, not floating. The Sundarbans is where both heritages converge.

### Aman — the architecture
Governs: pacing, sequence, information hierarchy. Don't announce, don't explain, don't seduce. Create conditions for discovery. The experience rewards attention proportionally — casual visitor gets beauty, careful one gets depth, obsessive one gets inexhaustible layers. Nothing shouts, everything is findable. Ruthlessly selective.

### Satyajit Ray — the craft
Governs: execution quality, economy of mark, polymath coherence. Every decision is considered. Figure-ground mastery: negative space as active architecture. Hand-and-system simultaneity: warmth without looseness, precision without sterility. Ray is the operating system — nobody sees it directly; they feel it in the fact that everything is considered.

---

## HOW THE PILLARS STACK

California is the site. Sundarbans is the material. Aman is the architecture. Ray is the craft.

Each layer is legible at a different level of attention:
- Casual visitor sees California (approachable, warm, clean)
- Curious visitor notices the Sundarbans (symbolic depth, the tiger)
- Engaged visitor feels Aman (pacing, reward structure, curation)
- Obsessive visitor recognizes Ray (every mark considered, polymath coherence)

## COLOR PALETTE — V2 (starting values, open to refinement)

| Role | Name | Hex | Character |
|------|------|-----|-----------|
| Light ground | Fog Pearl | #EDEBE6 | CA marine layer + dhaka muslin. The color of indirect light. Warm but not cream. |
| Mid neutral | Coastal Sage | #9EA393 | Relational — shifts warm or cool by adjacency. CA sagebrush + Bengal green. |
| Dark base | Indigo Black | #0C1119 | Near-black with indigo soul. Bengal dye trade. Reads black in body text, bleeds blue at scale. |
| Accent | Bengara | #8B3A2A | Iron oxide. Bengal → Japan (弁柄). Used sparingly — "passionate in specific moments." |

### Color usage principles
- Fog Pearl is the primary ground. Most of the site lives here.
- Indigo Black is the primary text color and secondary ground (for immersive sections).
- Sage is the connective tissue — metadata, secondary text, subtle UI elements. Never the star.
- Bengara appears RARELY. A hover state, a single word, a border. If it's everywhere, it's nowhere.
- The palette performs the personality: understated field, passionate in specific moments.
- Light sections feel California. Dark sections feel Sundarbans. The balance between them is the brand.

### Compression ladder
- 4-color: full system (portfolio, case studies)
- 3-color: fog pearl + indigo black + bengara (landing page, headers)
- 2-color: fog pearl + indigo black (favicon, minimal contexts)
- 1-color: indigo black (absolute minimum — the last survivor)

## TYPOGRAPHY — CANDIDATES (pressure testing, not locked)

### Primary sans candidates (choose one per test)
- **Untitled Sans** (Klim) — deliberately "undesigned," invisible craft, quiet
- **Söhne** (Klim) — more akzidenz-grotesk DNA, mechanical precision, authoritative
- **Suisse Intl** (Swiss Typefaces) — neo-grotesk structure, civic, orderly

### Mono candidates
- **Söhne Mono** (Klim) — mechanical, systems-thinking energy
- **Suisse Intl Mono** (Swiss Typefaces) — clean, conventional, functional

### Dark horse
- **Signifier** (Klim) — serif with visible ink traps at display, invisible craft at text. The Ray principle as a typeface.

### Typography principles
- "Serious optical intelligence hidden inside apparent simplicity."
- Faces where craft is invisible but felt.
- NOT ultra-thin geometric sans (empty minimalism).
- Type should feel pressed into the surface, not sitting on top of it.
- The italic is a tonal shift, not a different voice.
- Numerals matter — the portfolio has data-heavy case studies.
- Caption sizes (11-12px) must remain legible and warm.

### Type scale (starting point — adjust by feel)
- Display / Name: 48-72px, light or regular weight
- Section headers: 28-36px
- Sub-headers: 18-22px
- Body: 16-18px, generous line-height (1.5-1.65)
- Captions / metadata: 11-13px
- Code / data: mono, 13-14px
- Line lengths: 60-75 characters max for body copy

## MOTION PRINCIPLES

- Slow, tidal, inevitable. Nothing snaps or bounces. Things arrive.
- Long asymmetric easing. The way fog moves.
- CSS timing: ease-out or cubic-bezier curves with long durations (400-800ms for transitions, 200-300ms for hover states)
- Page transitions should feel like entering a different room, not loading a different page.
- Hover states reveal — they don't decorate. Something meaningful should change (content appears, color shifts, depth increases).
- Scroll behavior (where used): smooth, with sections that feel like breathing — dense then open, dense then open.

## COMPOSITION PRINCIPLES

### The Aman approach to web architecture
- The landing page is not a menu. It's an arrival.
- No "I'm Ashwin, a designer/builder/thinker at the intersection of..." — the work communicates identity.
- Show one thing well rather than proving breadth immediately.
- Threshold design: each section transition should feel like entering a different room with a different quality of light.
- Information hierarchy through absence — what's NOT there is as important as what is.

### The Ray approach to layout
- Figure-ground mastery: every element exists in relationship to the space around it.
- Negative space is active, not empty. It's the fog pearl doing atmospheric work.
- Don't categorize by medium. Present work as thinking made visible in different materials.
- Economy: if an element doesn't earn its place, remove it.

### Photography treatment
- Images should feel physically placed, not floating in a feed.
- Film photography: honor the grain, the color profile, the materiality of the medium.
- Composition should be immaculate on second viewing — don't crop carelessly.
- Natural light, shallow depth of field, material textures.
- Images at generous scale — don't thumbnail everything.

### Digital materiality
- Subtle texture where appropriate (not gratuitous noise overlays).
- Typography that feels pressed into the surface.
- Images that feel physically placed, not floating.
- Backgrounds that have luminosity, not flatness — fog pearl should glow slightly on a backlit display.

## LANDING PAGE DIRECTION

Currently exploring: a no-scroll landing page that functions as a room, not a menu.

### Principles for the landing page
- The visitor arrives in a composed space and chooses where to go.
- Navigation is spatial, not a hamburger menu or horizontal nav bar.
- Your name is the focal point.
- Work/content areas have some kind of presence — not just links, but visual or textual fragments that invite entry.
- Bengara appears only on interaction (hover, selection).
- The page communicates confidence through restraint.
- On mobile: may need a different composition that maintains the same feeling.

### Navigation options being explored
- A: Work as navigation — each project/section occupies a zone of the page
- B: Peripheral navigation — projects listed quietly along one edge
- C: Constellation — name as center, content areas arranged asymmetrically around it

## WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS NOT

This is not a rigid spec. It's a set of principles and starting values. The prototype should explore multiple expressions of these principles. Swap fonts, adjust colors, try different spacings, test different motion curves. The goal is to find what feels right through iteration, not to execute a predetermined design.

The brand promise — "there's always more underneath" — applies to the design process itself. The first version won't be right. The tenth version might be closer. The principles stay constant; the expression evolves.
